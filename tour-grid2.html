Kerala

Munnar

Munnar: A scenic hill station known for its sprawling tea plantations, Eravikulam National Park (home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr), and attractions like Mattupetty Dam and the Tea Museum.

Chennai Charms: Explore the Heart of Tamil Nadu
  • Tea Plantations: Explore the scenic landscapes of Munnar, nestled amidst sprawling tea plantations, misty hills, and cascading waterfalls, and learn about the region's tea cultivation and processing.
  • Eravikulam National Park: Discover the rich biodiversity of Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, and embark on a wildlife safari to spot elusive wildlife amidst lush greenery.
  • Mattupetty Dam: Marvel at the engineering marvel of Mattupetty Dam, offering panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys, and enjoy boating and picnicking amidst scenic surroundings.


Kerala

Alleppey

Alleppey (Alappuzha): Famous for its backwaters, where visitors can enjoy houseboat cruises, as well as the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race and Alappuzha Beach.

Backwater Bliss: Cruising Through Alleppey's Serene Waterways
  • Backwater Bliss: Embark on a mesmerizing houseboat cruise through the serene backwaters of Alleppey, surrounded by lush greenery, swaying palm trees, and tranquil waters.
  • Nehru Trophy Boat Race: Experience the thrill of the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, held on the Punnamada Lake in Alleppey, featuring traditional snake boats adorned with vibrant decorations.
  • Alleppey Beach: Relax and unwind on the golden sands of Alleppey Beach, offering panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and opportunities for leisurely strolls and water sports activities.


Kerala

Kochi

Kochi (Cochin): Known for its colonial-era landmarks like Fort Kochi, Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace), and the Chinese Fishing Nets along the waterfront.

Tea Estate Treasures: Discovering Munnar's Verdant Landscapes
  • Gateway to Kerala: Discover the historical and cultural significance of Kochi, also known as the Queen of the Arabian Sea, with its rich blend of Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences.
  • Fort Kochi: Immerse yourself in the colonial charm of Fort Kochi, adorned with picturesque streets, ancient churches, and iconic landmarks such as the St. Francis Church and Santa Cruz Basilica.
  • Chinese Fishing Nets: Witness the iconic Chinese Fishing Nets along the waterfront, a symbol of Kochi's maritime heritage, and experience the bustling activity of fishermen hauling in their daily catch.
