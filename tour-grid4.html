Agra

Agra: Home to the iconic Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World, as well as the Agra Fort, and Fatehpur Sikri, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Iconic Wonder: Exploring the Taj Mahal in Agra
  • Eternal Symbol: Marvel at the timeless beauty of the Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for its exquisite marble craftsmanship and eternal love story.
  • Agra Fort: Discover the grandeur of Agra Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and erstwhile seat of Mughal power, featuring impressive palaces, mosques, and courtyards.
  • Fatehpur Sikri: Explore the abandoned city of Fatehpur Sikri, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and admire its magnificent palaces, pavilions, and mosque, built by Emperor Akbar in the 16th century.

Varanasi

Varanasi (Benares): One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, known for its ghats along the River Ganges, Kashi Vishwanath Temple, and Sarnath, where Buddha gave his first sermon.

Spiritual Odyssey: Immersing in the Sacred City of Varanasi
  • Spiritual Sanctum: Experience the spiritual aura of Varanasi, one of the oldest continually inhabited cities in the world and a sacred pilgrimage site for Hindus.
  • Ganges Ghats: Witness the ritualistic fervor along the ghats of the River Ganges, where devotees perform sacred rituals, take holy dips, and attend mesmerizing Ganga Aarti ceremonies.
  • Sarnath: Visit the ancient site of Sarnath, where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment, and explore its Buddhist stupas, monasteries, and museum.

Lucknow

Lucknow: The capital city known for its rich cultural heritage, including landmarks like the Bara Imambara, Chota Imambara, and the British Residency, all reflecting the city's Mughal and colonial past.

Cultural Capital: Discovering the Heritage of Lucknow
  • City of Nawabs: Dive into the rich cultural heritage of Lucknow, known for its elegant architecture, mouthwatering cuisine, and graceful hospitality.
  • Bara Imambara: Explore the architectural marvel of Bara Imambara, an imposing complex built by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula in the 18th century, featuring the labyrinthine Bhool Bhulaiya and the grand central hall.
  • Chowk Bazaar: Wander through the bustling lanes of Chowk Bazaar, where you can shop for traditional chikan embroidery, exquisite jewelry, and delectable street food delicacies.
